Frequency (Hertz)

The frequency range varies from very low frequency
of $ 10^3 $ Hz to very high frequency of about $ 10^{22} $ Hz.

Buildings

A wave length with a very low frequency of about $ 10^3 $ Hertz, is a very long wave and is about as tall as an average building.

Humans

A wave length with a low frequency of about $ 10^6 $ Hertz,
is about as tall as an average human being.

Bee

The higher the frequency of a wave, the shorter the wave length. A wave with the frequency of $ 10^9 $ Hertz is about as long as a bee.

Pinpoint

A wave length with a frequency of about $ 10^{11} $ Hertz, is a very small wave and is about as big as a pinpoint.

Protozoans

A wave length with a frequency of about $ 10^{14} $ Hertz, is approximately in the middle of the spectrum, it is a tiny wave and about as big as a protozoan (which is a microscopic single selled organism.)

Molecules

A wave length with a high frequency of about $ 10^{17} $ Hertz, is a short wave and is about as small as a molecule.

Atoms

A wave length with a frequency of about $ 10^{20} $ hertz, is a very short wave and only about as big as an atom.

Atomic nuclei

Waves with a frequency of about $ 10^{22} $ hertz and beyond is extremely short waves, its length is about the same as that of an atomic nuclei.

Radio waves

These waves are the longest in the EM spectrum and vary from several hundred kilometres to a couple of millimeters. They are used for all kinds of things, from television and radio broadcasts to microwave ovens.

Visible light

The visible light spectrum is the part of the electromagnetic spectrum that is visible to the human eye.

Gamma rays and X rays

Gamma and X-rays have the same properties. Gamma rays just to a greater degree. X rays have high energy and can penetrate the human body.
