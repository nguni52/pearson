Calculating distance on topographic maps

There are two ways to determine straight line distance on 1 : 50 000 topographic maps. The first is to use the line scale and the second involves a simple calculation.

1. Line scale
To determine the straight line distance between the reservoir (A) and the golf course (B) on the map extract, follow these steps:

Step 1: Mark off the positions of the two places on a strip of paper.

Step 2: Lay the paper with the two marked places against the line scale.The left-hand side mark (A) must be aligned with zero on the scale.

Step 3: Read off the distance value where the second mark (B) lines up on the line scale.

From the line scale we can estimate that the straight line distance between the reservoir (A) and the golf course (B) is 4,5 km.

If the distance between your two marked places does not fall neatly between two divisions, you can use the subdivisions on the line scale to the left of zero to get a more accurate answer.

2. Calculation
The ratio scale of South African topographic maps is always 1 : 50 000. This means that 1 cm on the map represents 50 000 cm on the ground. But we never talk about a place being 50 000 cm away from another, so we need to convert 50 000 cm to metres or kilometres:
50 000 cm
= 500 m
= 0,5 km.
In other words we can therefore say that on topographic maps 1 cm represents 0,5 km.

To calculate the straight line distance between two points on a topographic map, we would follow these steps:

Step 1: Use a ruler to accurately measure the distance in centimetres between the two points.

Step 2: Multiply the number of centimetres by 0,5.

The formula we use to calculate any distances on topographic maps is: Distance on the map (cm) ×  0,5

Examples:

  • The distance between two points that are 10 cm apart on a topographic map is 5 km (10 cm× 0,5 = 5 km).
  • The distance between two points on a map that are 17,2 cm apart is 8,6 km (17,2 cm× 0,5 = 8,6 km).
