Interpreting orthophoto maps

Interpreting orthophoto maps:

  • Orthophoto maps are very similar to vertical aerial photographs, so you will use the same skills to help you interpret features on both types of maps.
  • Remember to use the same clues that you get from examining shadows, tone and texture.
  • Study the contours and the effects of tone and colour on the orthophoto map to understand the relief.
  • The large scale of the orthophoto map allows you to clearly see specific objects such as boats or planes. This will help you to identify land use.
  • Remember that natural features tend to be irregular in shape whereas constructed features are more regular and linear in shape.
