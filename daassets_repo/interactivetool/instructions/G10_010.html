Climate and GIS

Remote sensing techniques and GIS can be used to monitor the impact of climate change

  • Satellite remote sensing has provided major advances in understanding the climate system and its changes.
  • Remote sensing has helped scientists understand the spatial (size and shape) and temporal (time) changes of features such as the size of ice caps and sea level.
  • New insights are made by the unparalleled global coverage of satellite observations and improved resolution.
  • GIS technology has further enabled scientists to model and process this spatial data to predict future climate trends.
