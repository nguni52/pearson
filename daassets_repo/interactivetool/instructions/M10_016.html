Working with different types of photographs

As geographers, we often make use of photographs to help us understand the world around us. We work with different kinds of photographs, which include horizontal, oblique and vertical photographs.

Horizontal photographs are taken at ground level and show a view with which we are most familiar.

Oblique photographs are taken when the camera is tilted at an angle in relation to the ground. These photographs may be taken at ground level, from elevated positions or even from aeroplanes. High angle oblique photographs show the horizon, while low angle oblique photographs do not.

Vertical photographs are taken when the camera is perpendicular to the Earth’s surface and they show a bird’s eye view of the landscape.

Photographs that are taken from aircraft are called aerial photographs.
