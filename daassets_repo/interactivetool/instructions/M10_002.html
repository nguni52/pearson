Working with map scale

In the same way that model aeroplanes are scaled-down versions of real aeroplanes, maps are scaled-down graphic representations of reality.

Maps are drawn at various scales. The map scale tells us how many times smaller the map is than reality. It reflects the relationship (or ratio) between distance measured on the map and the corresponding distance on the ground. Most of the maps in this atlas show map scale in three different ways: ratio scale, line scale and word scale.

There is one other way that map scale can be expressed and that is in the form of a representative fraction.

1 9 000 000 is the representative fraction for the map that the other types of scale represent in the image above.

Small-scale maps, for example world maps, show less detail but cover large areas. Large-scale maps, on the other hand, show a lot of detail and cover a relatively small area as can be seen in this simplified school map.
