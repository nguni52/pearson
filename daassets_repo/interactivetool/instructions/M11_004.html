Comparing map scales

A map is a scaled-down or reduced diagrammatic representation of an area. The scale of a map tells us how many times smaller the map is than the area it represents. Large-scale maps show greater detail than small-scale maps, but cover smaller areas – think of the difference between a street map, which is a large-scale map, and a world map, which is a small-scale map.
