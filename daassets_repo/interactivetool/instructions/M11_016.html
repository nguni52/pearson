Identifying landforms and features on orthophoto maps

Orthophoto maps are vertical aerial photographs that have contour lines, place names and other conventional signs superimposed onto them. The scale of South African orthophoto maps is 1 : 10 000 and the contour interval is 5 m.

Colour orthophoto maps are easier to interpret but if you apply what you know about tone, shape, texture and shadows to black and white orthophoto maps, you will be able to recognise and identify a large variety of features.

As with topographic maps, orthophoto maps are given specific reference numbers. The area covered by one topographic map contains 25 orthophoto maps. The colour orthophoto is number 16 from block BA on the Rite topographic map (2930BA) and the black and white orthophoto is number 8 from block CB on the Pietermaritzburg topographic map 2930CB.
