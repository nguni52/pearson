Calculating magnetic declination and magnetic bearing

The magnetic north pole is situated west of true north and it is currently shifting westwards. This means that magnetic declination is increasing.

Magnetic declination information is displayed at the bottom of all South African topographic map sheets. We require this information in order to calculate present-day magnetic declination and to determine magnetic bearing.

Follow these simple steps in order to determine current magnetic declination:

Step 1: Determine the difference in years between the time of measurement and the current year.

Step 2: Calculate the total annual change.

Step 3: If migration is westwards, add the total change to the original declination. If migration is eastwards, subtract the total change from the original declination.
