Interpreting orthophoto maps, vertical aerial photographs and topographic maps

In addition to topographic maps and aerial photographs, geographers also use orthophoto maps to help them interpret and understand landscapes. Orthophoto maps are a combination of a vertical aerial photograph and a topographic map: contour lines, place names and other conventional signs are superimposed onto the vertical aerial photograph.

South African orthophoto maps have a scale of 1 : 10 000 which is five times larger than that of topographic maps and their contour interval is 5 m as opposed to 20 m. They are useful because they allow us to “zoom in” to an area on the topographic map in order to see more detail.
