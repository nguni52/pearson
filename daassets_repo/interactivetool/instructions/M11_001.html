Locating exact position on different kinds of maps

We use co-ordinates to indicate the exact position of places on maps. On small-scale maps, we may only make use of degrees (°) and minutes ('), but on larger-scale maps, such as topographic maps, we use degrees (°), minutes (') and seconds (").

For example, the co-ordinates for Reykjavik on the atlas map are 64°08'N; 21°55'W, but on a topographic map the co-ordinates would also include the values for seconds: 64°08'07"N; 21°55'43"W.
