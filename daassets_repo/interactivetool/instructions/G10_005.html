Remote sensing and the electromagnetic spectrum

Remote sensing and GIS

  • All remote sensing systems such as satellites depend on energy. Energy comes in many forms yet most traditional remote sensing systems measure wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation from within the electromagnetic spectrum.
  • We can only see a small portion within the electromagnetic spectrum, called visible light.
  • Computer processing enables us to interpret images from satellites.
  • Satellite images can be produced from a range of different sensors, each used for different purposes.
  • Spectral resolution refers to the range of wavelengths or bands in the electromagnetic spectrum detected by the sensors.
