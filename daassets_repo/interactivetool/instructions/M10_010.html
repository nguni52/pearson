Getting orientated on topographic maps

Topographic maps are medium-scale maps that are frequently used by Geography students. The map scale on South African topographic maps is ALWAYS 1 : 50 000. This means that 1 cm on the map represents 0,5 km on the ground. Each topographic map shows 15 minutes of latitude by 15 minutes of longitude (an area of about 640 kmÂ²). The actual map sheet is approximately 60 cm by 90 cm.

Pan around the reduced topographic map of Moorreesburg and identify the following features:

  1. Map reference number
  2. 1 minute of longitude
  3. Map scale (ratio)
  4. 1 minute of latitude
  5. Area of high relief
  6. Built-up area
  7. Transport networks
  8. Map scale (line)
  9. Reference columns showing conventional signs and symbols and their meanings
  10. Contour interval
  11. Map sheet numbers of neighbouring maps
  12. Location of map on map index, province in which the area on the map is located
  13. Magnetic declination information
  14. Distance to neighbouring settlement
  15. Agricultural areas

Also note that symbols showing vegetation are green, water features are blue and constructed features are grey, black, brown or red.
