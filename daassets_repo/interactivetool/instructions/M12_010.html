Interpreting cross-sections and calculating vertical exaggeration

Cross-sections give us a side view (profile view) of landforms. They help us to better understand the relief of a mapped area and to visualise relationships between different features. In order to show differences in relief more clearly, we usually exaggerate (enlarge) the vertical scale of cross-sections. The number of times we enlarge the vertical scale is called the vertical exaggeration.

In this cross-section, the horizontal scale (HS) is 1 : 50 000 and the vertical scale (VS) is 1 : 1 000. When we insert these values into the formula, we find that the vertical scale has been exaggerated 50 times.
