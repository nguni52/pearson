Steep and gentle slopes – contour lines

Gradient is the average steepness of slope or the angle of a slope.

A steep slope has a big difference in height between two places, for example a cliff as shown in photo A. A slope of average steepness generally has a gradient of approximately 45°, as shown in photo B. A gentle slope has a small height difference between two places.

Contour lines that are close together indicate a steep slope, whereas contour lines that are far apart indicate a gentle slope. Compare this physical map and contour map of a volcano. Note how the contour lines start off close together at the top of the volcano (steep slope) and get further apart at the bottom of the volcano (gentle slope).
