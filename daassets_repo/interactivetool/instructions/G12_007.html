GIS techniques – querying using maps

Querying is a way of selecting or filtering data, and is a very powerful capability of GIS.

If you’ve searched on the Internet then you are familiar with text searches. You can do the same type of query in a GIS. These are known as attribute queries and give you the same power as working in a conventional spreadsheet or database.

What makes GIS especially powerful is the ability to perform spatial queries, or queries based on location.

Some spatial relationships you can use in queries:

  • within or contains
  • touches
  • overlaps
  • intersects
  • distance.
