Identifying active and passive remote sensing

Active and passive remote sensing

  • Satellite and airborne sensors generally produce raster data.
  • Passive sensing senses radiation that is naturally emitted or reflected by Earth or the atmosphere.
  • Active sensing senses signals transmitted from a device and reflected back to it. Radar, lasers (LIDAR) and sonar are active sensing technologies.
  • Polar-orbiting satellites are placed between 500 km and 800 km above the Earth. It takes approximately 99 minutes for these satellites to make a single orbit around the Earth. These are also called lower-orbiting or low-earth-orbit satellites.
  • Geostationary or higher-altitude satellites are placed at 36 000 km and usually hover over the same area, as their orbit coincides with the rotation of the Earth so they orbit once every 24 hours. Weather-observing satellites are the best example of this type of satellite.
