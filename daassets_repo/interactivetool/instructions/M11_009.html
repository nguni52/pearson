Recognising slopes and landforms on topographic maps

On South African topographic maps, contour lines are brown. The interval between contour lines is constant at 20 m, and between index contours the interval is 100 m. The patterns that the contour lines make help us to identify individual features and to appreciate the relief of the mapped area.

The map extract shows an area dominated by a series of conical hills and pointed buttes with gentle-sloped, lower lying areas suitable for cultivation in between. The altitude ranges from 964 m to 1 238 m.
