Orienting aerial photographs and orthophoto maps with topographic maps

There is no value in using photographs to help you interpret the landscape seen in maps unless you can identify where the areas shown in the photographs are located on the map. Finding the location of the photographs on the maps is called orienting.

In order to orient the photographs with a map you must:

  • identify distinctive landmarks that appear on both
  • determine where north is in the photograph
  • turn the photograph upside down if necessary!
