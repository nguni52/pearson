Interpreting orthophoto map images from aerial photographs

To identify features on aerial photos and orthophoto maps, you can examine the shadows, tone and texture of features on the photographs to get clues about them.

  • Shadows help you to identify the height of features. The higher the features the longer the shadow. Shadows also help to identify the steepness of slopes.
  • Tone refers to the lightness and darkness of features in an image. Vegetation cover and water features are dark, whereas artificial surfaces reflect light and are lighter.
  • Texture describes the roughness or smoothness of features.

    • Also refer to the map of the same area to identify place names and features.
