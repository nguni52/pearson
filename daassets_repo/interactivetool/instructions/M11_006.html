Calculating the area of regular features

In order to calculate the area of a regular feature on a topographic map, we need to measure the length and width of the feature. It is important to remember to convert your length and width measurements to ground distances BEFORE inserting them into the formula: A = L × W.

Also remember that the map scale for topographic maps is 1 : 50 000, which means that 1 cm on the map is equal to 0,5 km in reality.

The area of the Pietermaritzburg city block outlined in yellow can be calculated as follows:

Step 1: L = 4 cm

Step 2: L: 4 × 0,5 = 2 km

Step 3: W = 1,5 cm

Step 4: W: 1,5 × 0,5 = 0,75 km

Step 5: A = L × W

Step 6: A = 2 km × 0,75 km
A = 1,5 km2
