Recognising relief features and contour patterns

Height is shown on maps by various conventional signs such as spot heights and bench marks, but also by contour lines. Contour lines are continuous lines on maps that join places that are at the same altitude. The patterns that contour lines make represent different landforms. We use these patterns to interpret the relief of the mapped area.

The distance between contour lines tells us about the gradient of slopes: where contour lines are close together, the slopes are steep and where they are far apart, the slopes are gentle.

The difference in height between two successive contours is called the contour interval.
