Understanding vertical exaggeration

When we construct cross-sections on maps we usually exaggerate (enlarge) the vertical scale in order to see the variations in relief (hills and valleys and slope details) more clearly. The number of times we enlarge the vertical scale is called the vertical exaggeration. The horizontal scale of the cross-section remains the same as the map scale, i.e. 1 : 50 000 on topographic maps and 1 : 10 000 on orthophoto maps.

We use the following formula to calculate vertical exaggeration:

VE = Vertical scale (VS) Horizontal scale (HS)

In order to calculate the VE of this cross-section, we need to first determine the VS and the HS that was used.

The VS is 1 cm : 20 m and the HS is 1 cm : 500 m.

We insert these values into the calculation and find that the VE is 25 times.

VS: 1 cm : 20 m = 1 cm : 2 000 cm

HS: 1 cm : 500 m = 1 cm : 50 000 cm

Therefore:

VE = Vertical scale (VS) Horizontal scale (HS)

VE = 1 : 2 000 1 : 50 000

VE = 1 2 000 × 50 000 1

VE =  25 times
