Conventional signs on topographic maps

At the bottom of every South African topographic map sheet, a standardised set of symbols is printed in order to help the user make sense of the area they are looking at. These standardised symbols are called conventional signs. Different colours are used to represent water (blue), vegetation (green), relief (brown) and constructed features (grey, red, black or brown).

The symbols usually take one of three forms as can be seen in the enlarged topographic map extract:

  • Point: monument, station
  • Line: railway line, road
  • Polygon/area: park, recreation.
