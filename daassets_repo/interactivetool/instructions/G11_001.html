Identifying types of spatially referenced data

Spatially referenced data

  • Spatially referenced data is also known as geospatial data or geographic information.
  • It is the data or information that identifies the geographic location of features and boundaries on Earth, such as natural or constructed features, oceans, and country boundaries.
  • A GIS gives you the ability to visualise and analyse spatially referenced data.
  • A spatially-referenced data set has a spatial component (i.e. coordinates) as well as non-spatial components (text, numeric or binary attributes).
  • It can be seen as a 'graphically-enriched database' or conversely as 'data-enriched graphics system'. In other words, it combines graphical maps and descriptive data in a very powerful way.
