Interpreting cross-sections of topographic maps

When cross-sections are constructed on topographic maps, the horizontal scale will always be 1 : 50 000. The vertical scale, however, varies from cross-section to cross-section. This means that the vertical exaggeration of cross-sections will also differ.

It is important to determine the ‘to’ and the ‘from’ place when constructing cross-sections. If you start at the incorrect point, you will produce a mirror image of the expected cross-section.
