Interpreting cross-sections from topographic maps

Cross-sections show us a side or profile view of landforms. They help us to see the changes of relief as one moves from one side of a feature to another. The cross-section through 30°S clearly shows that the altitude of the eastern side of South Africa is much higher than that of the west.

In Google Earth, the ‘show elevation profile’ tool allows you to view cross-sections of features anywhere in the world – go and experiment and explore!

Using the profile tool in Google Earth:

  1. Choose a feature in any location in the world.
  2. Click on the ‘Add path’ icon in the top toolbar, and name your path.
  3. Insert a path on the feature you have selected by clicking and releasing on the start and end points.
  4. Right click on the name of your path and choose ‘show elevation profile’ from the drop-down options.

A cross-section of your path will appear at the bottom of your screen.
